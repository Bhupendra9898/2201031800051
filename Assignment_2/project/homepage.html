Rose

1. Plant in well-drained soil with plenty of sunlight. 2. Water deeply but infrequently, avoiding wet foliage. 3. Fertilize regularly during the growing season. 4. Prune in early spring to promote healthy growth and blooming.

Tulip

1. Plant bulbs in well-drained soil. 2. Water moderately, keeping soil moist but not soggy. 3. Place in a sunny spot for optimal growth. 4. Remove faded flowers to promote new growth.

Mangolias

1. Plant in well-draining, acidic soil. 2. Water deeply but infrequently, especially during dry spells. 3. Provide full to partial sun exposure. 4. Prune after flowering to maintain shape and encourage new growth.

Marigold

1. Plant in well-drained soil in a sunny location. 2. Water moderately, allowing soil to dry between waterings. 3. Deadhead spent flowers to encourage continuous blooming. 4. Avoid over-fertilization to prevent leggy growth.

Mint

1. Plant in rich, moist soil with partial shade. 2. Water regularly to keep soil consistently damp. 3. Pinch back stems to promote bushier growth. 4. Divide plants every few years to prevent overcrowding.

Lotus

1. Plant in a large container or pond with plenty of room for growth. 2. Provide full sun and warm water for optimal growth. 3. Fertilize sparingly during the growing season. 4. Prune dead or yellowing leaves regularly to maintain plant health.

Jasmin

1. Plant in well-draining soil in a sunny location. 2. Water deeply and regularly, keeping soil evenly moist. 3. Provide support for climbing varieties. 4. Prune after flowering to maintain shape and promote new growth.

Lavender

1. Plant in well-drained soil and full sun. 2. Water deeply but infrequently, allowing soil to dry out between waterings. 3. Prune after flowering to encourage bushiness and longevity. 4. Avoid over-fertilization, as lavender prefers lean soil.

Cactus

1. Plant in well-draining soil in a sunny location. 2. Water sparingly, allowing soil to dry out completely between waterings. 3. Provide good airflow to prevent rot. 4. Avoid overwatering and fertilizing to prevent root rot.

Elovera

1. Plant in well-draining soil in a sunny spot. 2. Water deeply but infrequently, letting soil dry between waterings. 3. Provide good airflow to prevent rot, especially indoors. 4. Harvest leaves as needed for medicinal or cosmetic use.

Moneyplant

1. Plant in well-draining soil in indirect sunlight. 2. Water moderately, allowing soil to dry slightly between waterings. 3. Trim leggy stems to encourage bushier growth. 4. Keep away from direct sunlight to avoid leaf burn.

Tulsi

1. Plant in well-drained soil in a sunny location. 2. Water regularly, keeping soil consistently moist. 3. Pinch off flower buds to promote leaf growth. 4. Protect from frost and provide shelter during cold weather.

Orchid

1. Place your orchid in a location with indirect sunlight. 2. Water it sparingly, allowing the roots to dry between waterings. 3. Use well-draining orchid mix and avoid overpotting. 4. Provide occasional fertilization and repot every 1-2 years.

Mogra

1. Plant mogra in well-draining soil with full sunlight exposure. 2. Water regularly, keeping the soil moist but not waterlogged. 3. Prune regularly to promote bushy growth and remove dead flowers. 4. Provide support for climbing varieties and fertilize monthly during the growing season.

Lily

1. Plant lilies in well-draining soil with plenty of sunlight. 2. Keep the soil consistently moist but not waterlogged. 3. Remove faded flowers promptly to encourage more blooms. 4. Apply a balanced fertilizer during the growing season for optimal growth.

Fountain grass

1. Plant fountain grass in well-draining soil and full sunlight. 2. Water regularly, especially during dry periods, but avoid waterlogging. 3. Trim back dead foliage in early spring to encourage new growth. 4. Apply a balanced fertilizer in spring to promote healthy foliage and flowering.
